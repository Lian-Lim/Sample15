Description

Fire safety is the set of practices intended to reduce the destruction caused by fire. Fire safety measures include those that are intended to prevent ignition of an uncontrolled fire, and those that are used to limit the development and effects of a fire after it starts.

How to Prevent fire anywhere even your home!

1.Install Home Sprinkler Systems: If you install sprinklers in your home, the fire will be out over 96% of the time before firefighters arrive! They can be installed even after your home has been built..

2. Matches and Lighters are Tools Not Toys: In a child’s hand matches and lighters can be deadly Buy childresistant lighters and store all matches and lighters up high, where kids can’t see or reach them, preferably in a locked cabinet and Teach your children that matches and lighters are tools, not toys, and should be used only by adults or with adult supervision.

3.Cook Carefully: Never leave cooking unattended. Keep cooking areas clear of combustibles and wear clothes with short sleeves when you cookTurn pot handles inward on the stove, where you can’t bump them and children can’t grab them.

4.Keep an eye on Smokers: Careless smoking is the leading cause of fire deaths in North America Never smoke in bed or when you are drowsy Provide smokers with large, deep, non-tip ashtrays and soak butts with water before discarding them..

5.Crawl Low Under Smoke: During a fire, smoke and poisonous gases rise with the heat also The air is cleaner near the floor and If you must escape through smoke, crawl on your hands and knees to the nearest exit, keeping your head 12 to 24 inches above the floor.

By taking and listening to these items, it will help prevent fires and keep everyone safe.
